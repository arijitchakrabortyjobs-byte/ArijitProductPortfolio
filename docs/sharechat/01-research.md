# Research: ShareChat Creator Economy

## Where I Looked
I researched ShareChat's pain points across multiple sources: PissedConsumer (83 user complaints), Inc42 compliance reports showing 5-7 million monthly user complaints, Glassdoor employee reviews (740 reviews), G2/Product Hunt ratings (2.5/5 average), Reddit threads on r/india and r/startups, and industry analyses from Storyboard18 and Coherent Market Insights on India's creator economy.

## What I Found
The complaints clustered into four buckets: content moderation failures (40% of complaints were about abusive language), account security issues (hacking, unexplained bans, lost access), creator monetization gaps, and app performance/UX problems. The moderation volume was staggering — over 5 million user complaints in a single month with 2.9 million content takedowns.

## What Surprised Me
The biggest surprise was not the moderation problem — that's expected at ShareChat's scale. What surprised me was the creator side: fewer than 10% of creators on Indian regional platforms earn any meaningful income, and homegrown platforms like ShareChat capture only 5-10% of total ad spend compared to YouTube and Instagram. Yet ShareChat has 350 million MAU and 50,000+ active creators. The gap between platform scale and creator earnings visibility is enormous. Creators are posting into a void — they have no idea what they earn, which content works, or whether ShareChat is worth their time versus Reels or Shorts.

## Why I Didn't Pick Content Moderation
Content moderation is an ops and ML problem. It requires large trust-and-safety teams, classifier models, and policy frameworks. I can't meaningfully prototype that as a product thinker. But creator monetization transparency? That's a product problem — it needs dashboards, data pipelines, UX decisions, and prioritization. And it directly impacts ShareChat's biggest business risk: top creators leaving for platforms that give them better tools.
