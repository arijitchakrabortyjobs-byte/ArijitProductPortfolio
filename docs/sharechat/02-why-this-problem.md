# Why This Problem: Creator Earnings Visibility

## The Core Insight
ShareChat has a supply-side crisis hiding behind healthy DAU numbers. The platform has 31M+ daily active users and 1.38M active creators — but most of those creators have zero idea how much they're earning, from which sources, or whether their content strategy is working. This isn't a feature gap. It's a trust gap. And trust gaps cause churn.

## Why It Matters to the Business
I framed this problem through a business lens, not just a user empathy lens. ShareChat's monetization has reached a promising 50-50 split between ads and microtransactions (live gifts, coins). But none of this progress is visible to the creators who drive the content engine. If creators can't see a financial path on ShareChat, they'll default to YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels — which have superior analytics dashboards and established brand partnership ecosystems. Losing top creators means losing the content that retains users, which means losing DAU, which means losing ad revenue. It's a flywheel in reverse.

## What I Deliberately Chose NOT to Solve
I scoped out content moderation (ops/ML problem, not something I can prototype), payment/wallet systems (ShareChat already has payment infrastructure — the problem is visibility, not plumbing), live-streaming analytics (different UX context, deserves its own product stream), and brand/advertiser dashboards (the creator is the primary user for V1). Saying no to these was as important as saying yes to earnings visibility.

## My Hypothesis
If we give creators a transparent, real-time view of their earnings, content performance, and audience — they'll post more, stay longer, and eventually attract more brand deals to the platform. The metric I'd watch first: weekly active creators who check the earnings dashboard at least once. If that hits 80% within 3 months, we've built something creators actually need.
